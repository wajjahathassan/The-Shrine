About the Caretaker

I am Wajahat Hassan. I actually started out in Electrical Engineering. I spent a long time in that field, getting my Bachelor's and then my Master's. To be honest, I eventually realized that hardware wasn't really for me, but getting through those degrees taught me the most valuable thing I know: how to think like an engineer.

I learned how to be patient and how to break down big, scary problems into small, logical steps. Today, I use that same 'engineer's mind' when I'm coding in Python. I didn't leave my past behind; I just switched my toolkit from circuits to code to work on something I actually love.

Then, my career took a bit of a turn. I helped start SparkE Production, a small 3D animation company, along with four of my relatives. We didn't really have a boss. It was just the five of us trying to figure everything out together. I ended up doing a little bit of everything - from talking to clients on Fiverr to keeping track of deadlines and solving whatever technical problems came up.

Working with tools like Unreal Engine and Blender taught me that design is actually a lot like programming. I spent a lot of time fixing crashes, organizing files, and making sure we delivered good work on time. It made me realize that whether I'm building a 3D scene or a Python app, it's really about the same thing: having a good structure, using logic, and solving puzzles to make something cool.

You might notice I already have a Master's in Electrical Engineering. So, why go for another one? It's actually pretty simple: I want to specialize.

My background in engineering taught me how to work hard and how to solve difficult problems. But, I want to build software. And to do that the right way, I feel like I need the specific tools that you only get from a Computer Science degree. I don't really see this as starting over. It's more like, I have the foundation, but I need to go back and get that final piece so I can actually build the things I want to build.

Now, my goal is to build my future in South Korea, specifically around Asan and its neighboring cities. You might be wondering, why there?

Well, I already have a pretty strong connection to the area. My wife is currently a GKS scholar at a university in this region. Through her, I've seen firsthand that this whole area is a really unique mix of traditional history and massive high-tech industry.

Professionally, it just feels like the perfect environment for me to combine my engineering background with software. And personally, having my family within reaching distance would give me the stability and support system I need. It would mean I could stop worrying about the distance and just fully focus on doing my best work in the Master's program.
